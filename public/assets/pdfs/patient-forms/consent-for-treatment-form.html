CONSENT FOR TREATMENT FORM

Patient Information

Important Information About Your Healthcare

This form provides important information about your rights and responsibilities as a patient at Clinic 1 Medical. Please read carefully and ask questions if you need clarification.

General Consent for Medical Treatment

I understand and acknowledge that:

  • Medical treatment involves risks and no guarantee of cure or improvement can be made
  • My healthcare provider will explain recommended treatments, including risks, benefits, and alternatives
  • I have the right to ask questions about my care and treatment options
  • I have the right to refuse any recommended treatment
  • Emergency treatment may be provided without specific consent when delay would be harmful
  • Students or other healthcare professionals may be involved in my care under supervision

Specific Treatment Consents

Medication Information and Consent

I understand that:

  • All medications have potential side effects and risks
  • I should inform my healthcare provider of all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies I am taking
  • I should report any adverse reactions or side effects immediately
  • I should not stop prescribed medications without consulting my healthcare provider
  • Generic medications may be substituted unless I specifically request brand name drugs

Emergency Contact Authorization

Privacy and Confidentiality

Collection and Use of Personal Health Information:

Your personal health information is collected under the authority of the Health Information Act of Alberta and will be used for the following purposes:

  • Providing you with healthcare services
  • Billing Alberta Health Care and/or your insurance provider
  • Communicating with other healthcare providers involved in your care
  • Meeting legal and regulatory requirements
  • Quality assurance and improvement activities

Your health information will be disclosed to:

  • Healthcare providers directly involved in your care
  • Alberta Health Care for billing insured services
  • Your insurance provider for billing covered services
  • Laboratories and diagnostic facilities as required
  • Others as authorized by you or as permitted/required by law

Special Circumstances

Financial Responsibility

I understand and agree that:

  • Services covered by Alberta Health Care will be billed directly to the government
  • I am responsible for payment of services not covered by Alberta Health Care
  • I am responsible for any co-payments or deductibles required by my insurance plan
  • Payment is due at the time of service unless other arrangements have been made
  • I may be charged for missed appointments without adequate notice
  • I am responsible for providing current insurance information

Patient Rights and Responsibilities

As a patient, you have the right to:

  • Respectful, compassionate, and quality healthcare
  • Privacy and confidentiality of your health information
  • Participate in decisions about your care
  • Ask questions and receive clear explanations
  • Request a second opinion
  • Access your health records
  • File a complaint if you are dissatisfied with your care

As a patient, you have the responsibility to:

  • Provide accurate and complete health information
  • Follow agreed-upon treatment plans
  • Ask questions when you don't understand
  • Keep appointments or provide adequate notice of cancellation
  • Treat healthcare staff with respect and courtesy
  • Pay for services in a timely manner
  • Inform us of changes to your contact or insurance information
